Moana is a daughter of the cheif of her tribe. Coming from a long line of navidators

Moana[b] is a 2016 American animated musical fantasy adventure film produced by Walt Disney Animation Studios
and released by Walt Disney Pictures. The film was directed by John Musker and Ron Clements,
co-directed by Chris Williams and Don Hall, and produced by Osnat Shurer, from a screenplay written by Jared Bush,
and based on a story conceived by Clements, Musker, Williams, Hall, Pamela Ribon, and the writing team of Aaron Kandell and Jordan Kandell.

The film stars Dwayne Johnson and Auliʻi Cravalho in her film debut as the voice of the eponymous character. It features original songs written
by Lin-Manuel Miranda, Opetaia Foaʻi, and Mark Mancina, and an original musical orchestral score also composed by Mancina. The film is set in
ancient Polynesia and tells the story of Moana, the strong-willed daughter of a chief of a coastal village, who is chosen by the ocean itself
to reunite a mystical relic with the goddess Te Fiti. When a blight strikes her island, Moana sets sail in search of Maui, a legendary demigod,
in hopes of returning the relic to Te Fiti and saving her people. The screenplay takes inspiration from Polynesian myths.>
